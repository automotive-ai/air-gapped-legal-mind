Proposal for a EUROPEAN PARLIAMENT AND COUNCIL DIRECTIVE amending Council Directive 92/61/EEC relating to the type-approval of two or three-wheel motor vehiclesEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM1.

Aim of the proposalThe aim of this proposal is to facilitate the effective operation of the whole vehicle type-approval for two- and three-wheel motor vehicles and the uniform application by all Member States of the administrative and technical requirements relating to the type-approval.The experience gained since framework Directive 92/61/EEC relating to the type-approval of two- and three-wheel motor vehicles (1) entered into force has shown that certain requirements need to be clarified, in particular as regards the length of validity of the national approvals, and that the addition of harmonised requirements on the numbering of type-approval certificates, special provisions for end-of-series vehicles and the "new technologies", following the example of the regulations already in force in the motor vehicles sector, would be desirable.(1) OJ L 225, 10.8.1992, p. 72.2. Legal baseSince no provision is made for the amendment of the Articles of Directive 92/61/EEC by the procedure referred to in Article 16, and since the nature of the proposed amendments to the annexes of this same directive goes beyond that of a simple adaptation to technical progress, the Commission considers the forwarding of a proposal to the European Parliament and the Council to be justified.This proposal is based on Article 95 of the EC Treaty.This text is of interest for the EEA.3. General contextThe purpose of the Community type-approval procedure for the vehicles covered by the parameters of the framework directive is to ensure the effective operation of the single market through the introduction of uniform requirements with a view to obtaining the approval for a type of vehicle as well as the certification of its components and characteristics.Considering that road safety, environmental and consumer protection call for design and manufacturing requirements which are based on a high level and which must at the same time ensure market unity, this Community type-approval procedure for two-and three-wheel motor vehicles has been designed within a system of total harmonisation. It should thus be stressed that only vehicles, components and technical units which are in conformity with Community requirements may be placed on the market and sold for the first time for use in the Member States.Given the scale and impact of the proposed action in the sector in question, the Community measures provided for in this proposal are necessary, not to say essential, for the objective being pursued, namely Community vehicle type-approval, to be achieved. This objective cannot be achieved satisfactorily by the Member States acting separately.4. Contents of the proposalThe main aims of the measures proposed in Article 1 are:- to exclude from the scope of the acceptance procedure certain cycles with pedal assistance;- to clarify, in all the language versions of Directive 92/61/EEC, that the type-approval procedure does not apply to single vehicles;- to provide more precise definitions of the terms "variant" and "version";- to supplement the type-approval certificate by attaching thereto a document pertaining to the test results concerning noise and exhaust emissions, and to introduce a numbering system for this certificate;- to inform the competent authorities of the Member States more effectively of the approvals and certificates granted;- to inform the Member States and the Commission prior to any request aiming at having information other than that mentioned on the model presented in Annex IV section A included on the certificate of conformity;- to provide a better specification of the period of validity of the approvals and certificates granted at national level before the entry into force of the Community provisions; and- to introduce special dispensations for end-of-series vehicles as well as for vehicles, components and technical units designed according to techniques or principles which are by nature incompatible with certain specific requirements.This proposal also stipulates that the Member States should take the necessary measures for the transposition into national law to be effected before 1 January 2002, the date after which the Directive can be implemented on an optional basis and at the manufacturer's request. The proposed date for entry into force on a mandatory basis is 1 July 2002, which thus leaves a six-month period of optional application between the date of transposition and the date on which mandatory implementation commences.The main aims of the amendments listed in the annex of this proposal are:- to replace the information document by a new document containing more comprehensive information and on which the essential features are easily spotted;- to provide more detailed instructions concerning the documents to be annexed to the type-approval certificate;- to rule out any forging of the certificates of conformity;- to introduce a system of numbering of the type-approval and component type-approval certificates;- to add a document relating to the results of tests on noise and exhaust emissions; and finally- to add specific requirements governing the number of end-of-series vehicles authorised to be put into service in each Member State.5. Preparatory workThe proposed amendments have been examined by the Commission's "Motorcycles" Working Group in the course of several meetings that have been attended by experts from the Member States, the industry concerned and other interested sectors.
