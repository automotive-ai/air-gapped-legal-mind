WRITTEN QUESTION No. 1126/96 by Cristiana MUSCARDINI to the Commission. Type-approval of motor-vehicle components
WRITTEN QUESTION E-1126/96 by Cristiana Muscardini (NI) to the Commission (13 May 1996)Subject: Type-approval of motor-vehicle componentsThe need to harmonize type-approval procedures for motor vehicles, in addition to the need to reduce noise and atmospheric pollution levels and combat the practice of tampering, is the basis for the proposal for a directive COM(93) 449 final ((OJ C 177, 29.06.1994, p. 1. )).Two-wheeled vehicles would seem to offer a guaranteed solution in the fight against the traffic congestion which afflicts Europe's cities; however, the Commission's proposals in the directive are aimed in the opposite direction.As the manufacture of motor cycles and mopeds becomes increasingly out of reach economically, a growing percentage of the population are turning to the car.Can the Commission therefore take steps to amend the excessively low noise and emission limit values laid down in the directive for models currently on the market which would require major changes in the manufacturing process?Answer given by Mr Bangemann on behalf of the Commission (3 June 1996)As the Honourable Member says, the proposal for a directive on two or three-wheeled motor vehicles, which still need to be regulated for the implementation of the type-approval procedure, is necessary both to harmonize the diverging laws of the Member States and to ensure that the environment is adequately protected, both against air pollution and against noise pollution.Since the technological means of meeting the proposed requirements were available on the dates scheduled and the cost-benefit ratio was positive, the proposal received the backing of the majority of experts from the Member States and industry.The Commission would point out that most of the amendments proposed by Parliament at the first reading were accepted by the Commission and the Council in the common position adopted on 23 November 1995.
